Most critical item in this entire guide. Without SPF, DKIM, and DMARC, anyone on the internet can send an email that looks like it came from @eldib.com. Your anti-phishing policy protects inbound — these three records protect your domain reputation for outbound and block spoofing of your own domain.
SPF record — who is allowed to send email as @eldib.com
Where to add: Your domain registrar DNS settings (GoDaddy / Namecheap / Cloudflare / etc.) → Add TXT record for your root domain.
Record type
TXT
Host / Name
@ (root domain)
TTL
3600 (1 hour)
Value — M365-only sender
If all your email goes through Microsoft 365, this is the correct SPF record. Add other services (Mailchimp, Zendesk, etc.) with include: statements before the -all.
v=spf1 include:spf.protection.outlook.com -all
-all vs ~all: Use -all (hard fail) — it tells receiving servers to REJECT email from senders not in this list. ~all (soft fail) just marks them as suspicious. For Eldib & Co with only M365 sending email, -all is correct and stronger.
DKIM — cryptographic signature on outbound email
Where to enable: security.microsoft.com → Email & collaboration → Policies & rules → Threat policies → Email authentication settings → DKIM → Select your domain → Enable
Step 1 — Enable DKIM in Defender portal
Click your domain → Enable. The portal shows you two CNAME records to add to your DNS.
Enable in portal first
Step 2 — Add CNAME records to DNS
Add both records your registrar provides. These point to Microsoft's signing keys.
Host: selector1._domainkey.eldib.com
Value: selector1-eldib-com._domainkey.eldib.onmicrosoft.com

Host: selector2._domainkey.eldib.com
Value: selector2-eldib-com._domainkey.eldib.onmicrosoft.com
Step 3 — Verify DKIM is signing
Send an email to a Gmail account, open it → Show original → look for dkim=pass in the authentication results header
Verify with Gmail header
DMARC — policy that ties SPF + DKIM together
Enable DMARC AFTER SPF and DKIM are confirmed working. A wrong DMARC policy with p=reject before DKIM is ready will cause your own legitimate emails to be rejected.
Record type
TXT
Host / Name
_dmarc.eldib.com
Phase 1 — Monitor (none policy)
Start with p=none so you receive reports but no emails are blocked. Run this for 2 weeks and review the rua reports to identify all your legitimate sending sources.
v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@eldib.com; ruf=mailto:dmarc-reports@eldib.com; fo=1;
Phase 2 — Quarantine
After confirming all legitimate senders pass, switch p=quarantine. Suspicious emails go to spam instead of inbox.
v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; pct=100; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@eldib.com; fo=1;
Phase 3 — Reject (final goal)
Full enforcement. Any email that fails DMARC is rejected outright. No spoofing of @eldib.com is possible.
v=DMARC1; p=reject; pct=100; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@eldib.com; fo=1;
Free DMARC report analyzer: Use dmarcian.com or mxtoolbox.com/dmarc to parse the XML reports you receive at dmarc-reports@eldib.com — they are unreadable raw.

Attack Simulator is included in M365 Business Premium (Defender for Office 365 Plan 1). It lets you run real-world phishing simulations against your own users — without them knowing it's a test. Users who click get immediate training. This is the most direct way to measure and reduce human risk.
Set up your first simulation campaign
Path: security.microsoft.com → Email & collaboration → Attack simulation training → Simulations → Launch a simulation
1
Choose technique — Credential Harvest
Most common real-world attack. Fake Microsoft login page — user enters their password. You see exactly who fell for it and what credentials they submitted.
2
Select payload — Microsoft-themed
Use pre-built payloads that mimic real Microsoft notifications: "Your password is expiring", "Unusual sign-in detected", "Shared document" — highest click rates so most useful for training.
3
Target — All users, randomized delivery
Send to all users but stagger delivery over 2 days so it feels organic and users can't warn each other.
4
Assign training — auto-assign to users who click
Users who click the link immediately see a "you were phished" page and get auto-enrolled in a 10-minute training module. They complete it within 30 days — tracked in the portal.
5
Review results — click rate, repeat offenders
After 7 days, review the simulation report. Any user who clicks on 3+ simulations is a high-risk user — consider mandatory extended training or stricter CA policy (step-up auth).

Playbook — compromised user account
1
Contain — disable the account immediately
Entra ID → Users → select user → Block sign-in. This invalidates all active sessions within ~1 hour (or immediately if CAE is enabled).
2
Revoke all active tokens
Entra ID → Users → select user → Revoke sessions. This immediately kills all active access tokens for the user across all apps.
3
Reset MFA methods
Entra ID → Users → Authentication methods → Delete all MFA methods. Then re-register with the real user in person.
4
Investigate — what did the attacker access?
Entra ID Sign-in logs → filter by user → last 7 days. Defender portal → Incidents. Check Audit log for any mailbox rules, forwarding rules, or files accessed/downloaded.
5
Check for persistence mechanisms
Outlook → check for new inbox rules forwarding email externally. Entra ID → Enterprise apps → check for newly granted app permissions. Teams → check for new guest invitations sent.
6
Re-enable with fresh credentials
Force password reset. Re-register MFA in person or via video call. Re-enable the account. Monitor for 48 hours with elevated audit logging.
Playbook — ransomware on a device
1
Isolate the device immediately
Defender portal → Devices → select device → Isolate device. This cuts all network connections while keeping Defender telemetry active for investigation.
2
Do NOT pay the ransom
Since all data is in OneDrive (which you now have enforced), you can restore from OneDrive ransomware recovery + version history. The device itself can be wiped and re-enrolled from Intune.
3
Wipe and re-enroll the device
Intune → Devices → select device → Wipe (full wipe). All company data removed. Re-enroll via Autopilot. User's data is safe in OneDrive.
4
Restore OneDrive files
OneDrive web → Settings → Restore your OneDrive → select a date before the attack. All files restored to that point-in-time state.
